  • Part-time Analog/Mixed Signal IC Designer

    Position Description

    In this part-time position the candidate will be working in a team environment and involved in the CMOS SoC IC design process from specification to implementation and layout. The candidate must have deep and demonstrated understanding of analog and mixed signal circuit design, including methodology, layout, analog designer tools, processes and simulations and excellent knowledge of theoretical and practical fundamentals of analog IC. The candidate will be in intense interaction with the peer technical experts, so a significant responsibility and autonomy for an efficient interactive teamwork communication is required. Having at least 3 years of academic/industry experience designing a variety of analog, mixed-signal and digital circuits using advanced tools and foundry processes preferably in designing analog interfaces for low-noise, low-power biomedical sensors, RF transceiver and power management systems.

    Responsibilities

    • Work with a team of analog and digital designers on the design, simulation, testing and verification of both analog and mixed-signal circuits using industry best practices and participate in selecting and interfacing with any additional outside IC design groups
    • Help define IC requirements associated with the Biomedical SoC IC specifications/requirements, and create necessary design and product documentation
    • Develop transistor and/or system-level schematic and simulation design, layout, and silicon evaluation of interface circuits for the low-power biomedical application

    Requirements

    • Master or Bachelor’s in Electronics Engineering or equivalent
    • 3+ years of relevant experience in design, simulation, implementation, testing and verification of the analog/mixed SoC ICs
    • Good background in analog electronics and demonstrated experience as a designer for low-power and low noise complex analog or mixed-signal chips
    • Familiarity with industry standard design conventions and rules in analog circuit design; awareness of techniques to reduce design risk; preferably in CMOS 180nm and 90nm
    • Proficiency in design tools specially in Cadence: Schematic, Layout, DRC, Extraction, and LVS
    • In depth understanding of silicon manufacturing technology and experience debugging, probing and characterizing silicon using standard lab equipment
    • Creative, innovative, analytical mind and problem solver with ability to work independently with limited supervision
    • Good oral, verbal and writing communication skills and interact actively
